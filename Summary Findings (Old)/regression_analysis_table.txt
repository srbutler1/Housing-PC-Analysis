Table 1. Statistical Analysis of Housing Affordability Determinants (1996-2022)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Factor                                          Impact Mechanism                    Observed Effect
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Housing Supply Factors:
  Housing Starts                                Direct supply increase             Strong positive
  Construction Investment                       Indirect through supply            Moderate positive
  Construction Prices                           Cost pressure on supply            Strong negative

Demand-Side Factors:
  Population Size                               Base demand driver                 Moderate negative
  Household Income                              Purchasing power                   Strong positive
  Unemployment Rate                             Income stability                   Strong negative
  
Financial Conditions:
  Mortgage Rates                                Cost of financing                  Strong negative
  Consumer Price Index                          Real income erosion               Moderate negative
  
Economic Environment:
  GDP Growth                                    Overall economic health            Moderate positive
  Exchange Rates                                Import costs/Investment            Weak negative
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Key Relationships Identified:

1. Supply-Side Dynamics:
   - Housing starts showed strongest positive impact during recovery periods
   - Construction prices gained significance post-2008
   - Investment effects show time-lagged positive impact

2. Demand Influences:
   - Population effects stronger in urban areas
   - Income effects amplified during crisis periods
